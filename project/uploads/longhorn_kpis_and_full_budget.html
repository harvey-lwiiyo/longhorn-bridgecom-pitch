Longhorn Insurance Brokers — Calibrated KPIs, Industry Benchmarks and Full Agency Budget

KPI philosophy
We promise what the industry average delivers.
We plan to significantly exceed it.

Every KPI presented to Longhorn in this document is set at or below global industry average benchmarks for financial services in emerging markets. This is deliberate. The targets we commit to publicly are the floor. The targets we work to internally are materially higher. This protects the client relationship, gives BridgeCom room to demonstrate genuine over-performance, and ensures every milestone reported is a credible, comparable number rather than an optimistic projection.

Internal working principle: The numbers in the "Our commitment" column are what we put in the contract and report against every month. The numbers in the "Internal stretch" column are what the team works to hit. If we meet our commitment but miss our internal stretch, that is still a good month. If we meet both, that is a great month we report with context. If we miss our commitment, that is a serious conversation with a clear recovery plan.

How we set the benchmarks
Source data used

Global benchmarks from WordStream (1,000+ campaigns), Sprout Social (cross-network 2025 data), LinkedIn official CPL data for financial services, Flyweel CPL index (50,000+ businesses), and Deloitte Africa Insurance Outlook 2024/25. Adjusted downward by 40 to 60% for emerging market pricing, which consistently runs at a fraction of US and UK rates due to lower advertiser competition and lower digital ad market maturity.

The emerging market adjustment

Research from LeadSync and WordStream confirms that leads from emerging markets (including sub-Saharan Africa) cost 2 to 3x less than US and UK equivalents. Zambia sits in a particularly low-competition digital advertising environment, which means CPLs will be lower than even regional African averages in the early months before the market matures. We use South African benchmarks as our upper ceiling and adjust down for Zambia's lower competition environment.

How to read the KPI tables
Global industry average
What financial services brands achieve globally. This is public benchmark data. Verifiable by any third party.
Our commitment to Longhorn
What we put in writing. At or slightly above the emerging market adjusted average. Conservative. Credible. Contractual.
Internal stretch target
What BridgeCom's team works to achieve. Not shared with the client in reporting. The bar we hold ourselves to internally.

Why this matters commercially: An agency that over-promises and under-delivers loses the client at month three. An agency that under-promises and over-delivers earns trust, earns renewals, and earns referrals. The latter is the only commercially sustainable model. These KPIs are built for the long relationship, not the winning pitch.

Why LinkedIn leads justify the higher cost

A single LinkedIn lead that becomes a corporate group policy generates K 40,000 to K 120,000+ in annual premiums through Longhorn. Even at our commitment CPL of ZMW 2,500, converting one in ten LinkedIn leads to a consultation and one in four consultations to a policy means each policy costs approximately ZMW 100,000 to acquire, against revenue of K 40,000 to K 120,000 per year. The CPL is never evaluated in isolation. It is evaluated against the lifetime value of the client it produces.

Full agency budget
Every cost. Transparent and justified.
Nothing hidden. Nothing assumed.

This budget covers everything required to execute this plan from a zero starting point through the first twelve months. It is organised into five categories: one-off setup costs (paid once), monthly retainer services (ongoing agency fees), technology and tool subscriptions, advertising media spend (paid directly to platforms), and production costs. All figures are in Zambian Kwacha (ZMW). Exchange rate basis: 1 USD = approximately ZMW 28.

How to use this budget: The setup costs are charged in Months 1 and 2. The monthly retainer begins from Month 2. Advertising spend begins from Month 3. Total year-one investment is the sum of all categories. The client retains ownership of all accounts, all creative assets, the website, and the CRM at all times. If the engagement ends, nothing is held by BridgeCom.

The return case in plain numbers

At our commitment KPIs, 5 to 10 policies signed per month by Month 12, with an average annual premium of K 15,000 per retail policy and K 60,000 per corporate policy, the monthly premium revenue from digital-sourced clients at our commitment target is K 80,000+ by Month 12. That is K 80,000 in new monthly revenue generated by a programme costing K 51,652 to K 86,422 per month. Break-even on the retainer happens within the programme's own output. Everything added through referrals, renewals, and organic compound growth after that is the long-term return.

Payment terms and commercial model
Retainer terms

Monthly invoicing. Minimum engagement: 6 months (brand growth takes time; 3-month retainers produce neither results nor learning). Payment due within 15 days of invoice. Ad spend invoiced separately from agency fees and paid directly to platforms by client for full transparency.

What the client owns

All social media accounts and page admins. The website design files. All CRM data and contacts. All content assets produced during the engagement. All advertising accounts. If the engagement ends, Longhorn takes everything with them. BridgeCom holds nothing.

Performance review

Monthly review meeting with Longhorn leadership (30 minutes minimum). Quarterly full strategy review (90 minutes, CEO present). If committed KPIs are not met for two consecutive months, BridgeCom presents a formal recovery plan before the third month begins. No hiding. No excuses. A plan.
